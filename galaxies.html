1. IC 1101

Still the champion, this supergiant elliptical boasts a mind-blowing diameter of 4 million light-years, dwarfing our Milky Way by a factor of 40. It's a behemoth filled with old stars and harbors the biggest black hole ever discovered.

2. Hercules A

Another supergiant elliptical galaxy, a titan at 1.5 million light-years wide, which is roughly 15 times the size of our Milky Way.

3. Milky Way

Our home galaxy! A barred spiral galaxy estimated to be around 100,000 light-years across, teeming with billions of stars, our solar system included.

4. A2261-BCG

Rounding out the list, this supergiant elliptical galaxy stretches a million light-years wide, making it 10 times larger than our Milky Way. Interestingly, it seems to be missing a supermassive black hole at its core, an anomaly among giant galaxies.
